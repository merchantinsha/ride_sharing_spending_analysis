# Impact of Ride-Sharing & Food Delivery Apps on Student Spending

Analysed survey data from **200+ students** to understand how ride-sharing (Uber, Ola) and food delivery apps (Swiggy, Zomato) affect spending habits, budget management, and financial behaviour among young adults.

&nbsp;

## Objective

Investigate three key questions:

1. How do students manage their budgets — parent-funded vs self-funded?
2. How frequently do students use ride-sharing vs food delivery apps?
3. Is there a correlation between spending on rides and spending on food delivery?

&nbsp;

## Key Findings

- **Majority of students are parent-funded**, which influences how freely they spend on convenience apps.
- **Food delivery apps are used more frequently** than ride-sharing — contributing more to weekly spending.
- Students who spend more on food delivery also tend to spend more on ride-sharing, suggesting a **positive correlation** between the two.
- Most students fall in the **₹0–₹1,000 monthly range**, with a few outliers spending ₹3,000+.

&nbsp;
